Structure

Page chrome

Running header and page number are the only marks that sit outside the content area. Tiny, mono, ink-faint — present enough to orient, quiet enough to disappear. Standard content, TOC, comparison, and colophon carry both; cover and chapter divider drop both; stat-anchor and pull-quote keep the page number only.

ElementSpecPosition
Running header mono 8pt · ink-faint · +0.04em 12pt above top trim. Left: document name. Right: current chapter title.
Page number mono 9pt · ink-faint · tabular-nums 12pt below bottom trim. Outside edge — right on recto, left on verso (portrait); right corner (landscape).
Margin rule (optional) 1px · ink-faint A hairline between header and body, only on documents over 40 pages — gives long docs a stronger orienting line.

Unordered · em-dash bullet

  • A hypothesis is a prediction with a fail condition — metric, magnitude, timeframe.

Ordered · numbered, accent

  1. State the signal in one sentence.

  3. Name the cheapest test that could prove the hypothesis wrong.

Quantitative limits

Density limits

A document violates Quire when any of these limits is exceeded. They prevent the most common AI failure mode: treating every page as if it were the most important page.

Element Limit Why
Callouts (Exercise / Note / Think) ≤ 2 per spread Three or more makes the page feel like an annotated lesson, not a chapter.
Pull-quote pages ≤ 1 per chapter Pull-quotes punctuate. Two in a chapter becomes decoration.
Stat-anchor pages ≤ 2 per document Stats anchor; over-use turns the document into an infographic.
Accent surface (per page) judgment call The 5 % cap was retired. The principle holds: one accent should still feel scarce.
Inline .hl highlights ≤ 2 per page Highlight everything = highlight nothing.
Visual reset (cadence) every 5–7 content pages Beyond seven, readers lose orientation in long documents.

11 / 12

Output

Print rules

Constraints that only bite when exporting to PDF for press or bind. Screen- only documents may ignore them; anything destined for paper cannot.

ConstraintValueWhy
Bleed 3 mm all sides Required on covers, chapter dividers, and any full-bleed figure. Extend the background past the trim line.
Safe area 5 mm inside trim No text or critical mark closer than 5 mm to the trim — binding tolerance eats the rest.
Page-break inside avoid Callouts, stat-blocks, figures, and compare tables must not split across pages.
Widows & orphans ≥ 2 lines A single trailing or leading line of a paragraph reads as a typesetting error.
Hyphenation off in headings; on in body Headings break by sense, not syllable. Justified body reads better with hyphens on.
Image resolution ≥ 300 dpi at print size Below 300 dpi, detailed images soften visibly once bound.
Color space sRGB authoring · CMYK soft-proof Sky-blue accent shifts on press; check the proof before committing to a print run.

12 / 12

Hard rules

The 10 invariants

The rules Quire will not break. Each has a real cost — think before overriding any of them.

  1. Cool canvas #f6f8fb, never pure white, never a warm cream.
  2. Sky blue #3a82c4 is the only chromatic color — no second hue.
  3. All grays cool-toned (B ≥ G ≥ R). No warm yellow-brown grays.
  4. One serif family carries hierarchy. Sans only for UI chrome.
  5. Two weights only: 400 / 500. No bold, no italic body.
  6. Typography mechanics: titles tight, eyebrows loose, body zero.
  7. Chapter dividers use --accent-tint, never full-saturation.
  8. No shadows, no gradients, no blur. border-radius ≤ 3pt.
  9. No personal-brand chrome — no avatars, badges, signature boxes.
  10. Every callout has a label; unlabelled blockquotes drift into decoration.

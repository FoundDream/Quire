# Quire · Content Strategy

How to write the prose that fills a Quire document. Good typography on bad content is "polished mediocrity". This file covers the writing voice; layout rules live in `design.md` and `components.md`.

---

## The voice

Quire documents read like **thoughtful operators talking to other operators**: confident, specific, willing to take a position, allergic to filler.

Reference voices:
- a16z guides — declarative, pattern-named
- Stripe Press essays — long-form, footnoted, opinionated
- Ben Thompson / Stratechery — analytical, framework-driven
- The Economist — third-person, declarative, no hedging

What the voice is not:
- LinkedIn thought-leader (over-emphatic, formulaic)
- Marketing brochure (adjective-heavy, vague)
- Academic paper (passive, hedge-heavy)
- Tutorial / how-to (procedural, pedestrian)

## Sentence-level rules

### Active voice, present tense

> ✗ The codebase will be generated by Claude Code based on the architectural decisions that have been made.
>
> ✓ Claude Code generates the codebase from the architectural decisions you've already made.

### Cite numbers, not adjectives

> ✗ A surprisingly large percentage of startups fail because they build the wrong thing.
>
> ✓ 42% of startups fail because they build something nobody wanted.

If you don't have the number, name the source. If you can't name the source, cut the claim or hedge it explicitly ("anecdotally," "in our portfolio").

### Concrete subjects

> ✗ It is important that the right tools are used at the right time.
>
> ✓ Founders pick the wrong tool when they default to the one they know.

### One claim per sentence

If a sentence has two `and`s or a comma-spliced second clause, split it.

### Short paragraphs

Three sentences average. Five sentences max in body. One-sentence paragraphs are allowed for rhythm.

## Paragraph-level patterns

### The chapter opener

Every chapter opens with a **lead paragraph** in the `.lede` style. Three jobs:

1. State the chapter's claim in one sentence.
2. Name the founder's pain in concrete terms.
3. Hint at the resolution this chapter delivers.

Example:

> Every founder starts from the same place: a problem they can't stop thinking about. This is the phase where idea meets reality — and where startup success in 2026 requires the discipline of not building until the evidence justifies it.

### The "challenge → antidote" pattern

Quire chapters tend to follow this structure for each major risk:

```
The challenge: [one-sentence problem statement]
[2–3 paragraphs explaining why it's hard]
The antidote: [one-sentence solution direction]
[2–3 paragraphs explaining how to apply it]
[A concrete Exercise callout]
```

Use `<strong>` on the lead phrase ("The challenge:" / "The antidote:") to anchor the eye.

### The "stage exit criteria" pattern

For multi-stage playbooks, every stage chapter ends with an explicit checklist:

> You're ready to leave the Idea stage when you can answer yes to all three:
>
> 1. Is the problem real and specific?
> 2. Does your solution address the actual problem?
> 3. Do you have enough signal to justify building?

Numbered list, parallel grammar, ≤ 5 items.

## Callout writing

| Type | Voice | Length | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exercise** | Imperative, concrete action | 2–4 sentences | "Work with Claude to sharpen your problem statement until it's testable…" |
| **Note** | Quiet, parenthetical | 1–2 sentences | "Note: Using Claude as devil's advocate is a core use case at every stage." |
| **Think** | Reflective prompt | 1 sentence | "Think: would you bet your savings on this hypothesis?" |
| **Warning** | Pre-mortem, naming the failure | 2–3 sentences | "Warning: shipping before security review is the most common pre-launch breach." |

Every callout has a label. Unlabelled blockquotes are pull-quotes (different component).

---

## Pull-quote selection

A pull-quote should be:
- A claim, not an observation
- Quotable on its own (passes the "tweet test")
- Short — 25 words max
- From a named source (book, podcast, person) or attributed to the document itself

Bad pull-quote:
> "AI is changing how startups work."

Good pull-quote:
> "The founder's role becomes much less individual contributor, much more orchestrator of agents."

One pull-quote per chapter, max. Two becomes decoration.

## What to cut

Quire's editor-mode pass should remove:

- **"In today's world, …"** — every variation of this opener
- **"It is important to note that…"** — say the thing
- **"Many people believe…"** — name who, or cut
- **"Game-changer"**, **"revolutionize"**, **"unlock"**, **"empower"** — marketing residue
- **"Best practices"** unless followed by exactly 3 named practices
- **Adjective stacks** — "innovative, scalable, seamless solution" → pick one or none
- **Rhetorical questions in body** ("So what does this mean?") — make the claim directly
- **Filler transitions** — "That said, …", "With that in mind, …"
- **Emoji** — Quire is bound, not chatted

---

## Numbers and units

- Numbers under 10: spell out ("three founders")
- Numbers 10+: numerals ("12 founders")
- Always use tabular-nums in tables and stat blocks
- Currency: locale-aware, full unit on first mention ("$50K ARR", later "ARR")
- Percentages: numeral + symbol, no space ("42%")
- Time ranges: "3+ days", not "3 or more days"

---

## Footnotes and citations

For multi-page Quire documents, prefer **inline citations** over footnotes:

> ✓ Per CB Insights' 2024 startup post-mortem analysis, 42% of failures…
>
> ✗ 42% of failures (1) … then a footnote at page bottom.

If a footnote is unavoidable (legal, technical), use superscript numerals + a `.notes` block at chapter end, not page-bottom — page-bottom footnotes break the cream rhythm.

## Localization (Chinese)

When writing the Chinese version of a Quire document:

- Body text: 使用细黑、思源宋体、TsangerJinKai02 等带有暖调的衬线，与英文 Fraunces 视觉等价
- Letter-spacing: 中文 body 加 0.1–0.2pt（中文字符天然密集，需要呼吸）
- Avoid 1:1 translation — re-write in Chinese editorial cadence (短句、动词主导)
- 严禁 ChatGPT 式中文翻译腔（"在某种程度上"、"值得一提的是"、"综上所述"）
- Numbers and accent color rules transfer unchanged

## A pre-publish checklist

Before exporting to PDF, run through:

- [ ] Every chapter opener has a lead paragraph with one operative claim
- [ ] No "in today's world / it's important to / game-changer" residue
- [ ] All numbers cite a source or are explicitly anecdotal
- [ ] Every callout has a label
- [ ] Pull-quotes pass the tweet test
- [ ] Exit criteria lists have parallel grammar
- [ ] No emoji, no rhetorical questions, no adjective stacks

Chapter 01 · The Problem

Word document gray is not neutral — it's stale

AI can produce a 50-page playbook in minutes. Most of them land as gray Calibri walls on pure-white pages — technically readable, but nothing about the page invites you to keep reading.

Why this happens

Without a constraint system, every session drifts toward defaults — system fonts, blue-gray neutrals, no rhythm. The output looks like a Word document because that's what training data is full of.

What's missing

Not capability. Not effort. The missing piece is discipline: a small set of rules that an agent will not break, no matter how the prompt drifts.

Note Polished typography on bad content is "polished mediocrity." Quire's job is the typography. You bring the argument.

5%

The maximum surface area Quire allows for any accent color, across the entire document.

More than that and the document reads as a marketing brochure, not an editorial book. Below 5%, restraint becomes the brand.

Type, spacing, cadence

Default document vs. Quire

Same content, different rules. The defaults are not neutral — they encode decisions that fight against editorial reading.

Element Default doc Quire
Canvas Pure white #ffffff Cream #faf9f5

Grays Cool blue-gray (R = G = B) Warm yellow-brown (R ≥ G > B)
Accent colors Multi-color, no ceiling One accent, ≤ 5 % surface

Chapter 03 · The Page Archetypes

Seven archetypes, one rule of cadence

Every page in a Quire document belongs to one of seven archetypes. The discipline isn't picking the right one — it's knowing when to break the rhythm.

The archetypes

  • Cover — page 1, full-bleed accent or cream, oversized title
  • TOC — page 2, eyebrow + numbered list with leader rules
  • Chapter divider — lightened-accent tint, chapter number + title only
  • Standard content — most pages, h1/h2/h3, body, lists, callouts
  • Stat-anchor — used 1–2× per document, oversized figure as hero
  • Comparison — three-column compare table introducing a trade-off
  • Pull-quote — half-page editorial quote, max one per chapter

The cadence rule

Every 5–7 content pages must contain a visual reset: a chapter divider, a pull-quote page, or a stat-anchor. Without it, readers lose orientation. With it, they skim productively — and come back to the parts that earn their attention.

Exercise Count pages between resets in your last long-form document. If any stretch goes over seven content pages, you've found where readers stopped.

Colophon

This playbook was set with Quire.

Twelve pages, one accent color (clay #d97757), one serif family (Fraunces), one sans for chrome (Inter Tight). Eleven content pages plus this colophon. Page count is even; covers and dividers fall on odd pages.

Quire is a constraint system, not a generator. It can compose this document because the document follows its own rules. If a Quire-styled output ever breaks these rules, the rules — not the document — are wrong.
